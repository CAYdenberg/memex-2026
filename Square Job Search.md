## Standard cover letter

To whom it may concern,

I am a frontend developer who has been building the web for eleven years. I have worked for graphics design agencies, for product companies, and as a freelancer, where I was often the sole developer and responsible for everything from design to database. I have been working with React almost since its initial release, and have been responsible for several very large codebases: seeing the many ways they can go right and wrong at scale. I am believer in high-quality, self-documenting code, and am generally of the opinion that more tests and stronger typing save time in the long run.

I am interested in Holepunch because I think that web development is reaching a dead-end. The server-client architecture that most modern SaaS apps use is often criticized for taking ownership of users' data, but I think the problem is much worse. It stifles innovation into tools that would open up truly collaborative workflows for knowledge workers outside of software.

Most importantly, I have a long history of productive relationships with designers, product managers, and users. When I was freelancing, I was successful because I worked constantly with people from a variety of technical backgrounds to understand their pain points. This has given me a keen sense of UX design and an understanding of how to put the user first.

Yours,
Casey Ydenberg

**Do you have experience building desktop applications (Electron, Tauri, or similar)?

Some years ago, I contributed some code to ScienceFair, which was an open source Electron app that used Dat to share scientific papers directly between researchers.

**Do you have experience writing modular, reusable UI components and design systems?

I maintained a pattern library as part of the Manuscripts.io ecosystem. Additionally, while at CommunityLogiq, I restructured large parts of the codebase to make better use of render props to allow the components to be usable in different contexts.

**Do you have knowledge of real-time communication technologies (WebRTC, media streams, WebSockets)? 

Not directly, but I have used PouchDB at two different jobs, and those integrations included real-time reactions to synced database changes.

**Briefly describe a frontend project you've worked on that you're particularly proud of. 

As a side-project during COVID, I built a data visualization pattern library (https://hypocube.livingpixel.io/). It featured full SVG-canvas interoperability, a novel approach to responsive charts, and gesture-based interactivity. While I failed to generate any interest in the project from the web community, working on it was a great joy, and I personally have used it to build all kinds of different charts over the years.
