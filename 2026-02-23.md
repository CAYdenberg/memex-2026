### Subscription flow

1. Account Holder signs up. Will Accept Terms and Conditions on sign-up.
2. Account Holder is directed to invited to subscription page (via a toast) immediately after sign up
3. If they choose *not* to subscribe, they can use the app on a limited basis (up to one week OR up to 5 reports?)
4. If they choose to subscribe, they are directed to a Company management page, where they can create a company (inc. metadata eg Company Name), but can also add and remove users from the company (tracked as references within Firebase).
5. A special field in the Company record tracks the user who created it
6. Any user added will receive an email inviting them to sign up. However, these won't be handled as "invitations" in the existing sense.
7. If a user is invited, we create a new User in Firebase with *no password* (need schema change), and a reference to the Company.
8. When that user signs up, we simply add them in Firebase Auth, but don't modify their record at all

### Other changes needed (probably not for round #1)

1. If a Company Owner needs to add more seats, fewer seats, or change or cancel their subscription, we will have to do that through the [customer portal](3. If a Company Owner needs to add more seats, fewer seats, or change their subscription, we will need to direct them to Stripe's Customer Portal) until we have time to build out our own UI 
2. Whenever a user takes any action that requires a subscription, we have to check their *Company*. This effectively means moving logic from frontend to backend, but can be an ongoing process.
3. If a user is *removed* from a company, the reference to their Company is removed from their User. This means they effectively lose their subscription. They can be warned with an email (and then prompted to create their own). But they would keep their account/password (desirable).
4. If a subscription lapses due to an invalid transaction, they'll be warned by an email from Stripe and they can manage/fix it via the customer portal.
5. We'll want to cache subscription checks (calling Stipre API) before we grow too big
6. Probably will need to handle users who are part of multiple companies, too
7. Will need to refine the calls to action and prompts to create subscription on an ongoing basis
